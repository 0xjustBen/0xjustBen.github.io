DOSSIER 02

          
From red-team telemetry to detection rules — automatically.

          
An LLM-assisted pipeline that ingests Sysmon / EDR traces from emulated TTPs and emits draft Sigma, KQL and YARA rules with negative tests included. Tuned with a critic that re-runs the technique with light variations (noise injection, parent-PID swaps) and rejects rules that miss the variants. Cuts the gap between an emulated technique and a deployable detection from days to minutes.

DOSSIER 03

          
Disposable C2 infrastructure — built, aged and burnt by pipeline.

          
A reusable kit that spins up a hardened team-server (Cobalt Strike, Mythic, Sliver, Havoc) and a layered redirector chain that rides on legitimate Azure & AWS services — App Service, Front Door, API Gateway, CloudFront, Lambda — instead of CDN domain-fronting. The whole stack is IaC-driven (Terraform + Ansible, GitHub Actions): provisions infra, registers domains across registrars, ages them with low-volume traffic, serves benign content until activation. Each engagement gets fresh, never-reused infra with reproducible profiles, OPSEC linters on every beacon, and a destroy job that wipes everything down to DNS and billing.

DOSSIER 05

          
EDR evasion, tailored to how the EDR actually hooks.

          
A continuously-updated lab that benchmarks evasion primitives against the EDRs I see in production. The technique is chosen per target: against userland-hooking products I focus on callstack masking, sleep-mask & thread-stack spoofing, indirect syscalls and module stomping; against kernel-mode telemetry the focus shifts to ETW/AMSI patching, kernel-callback awareness and sensor-aware behaviour. Each technique ships with detection notes for the purple-team side — nothing here lands without a corresponding rule.
